SECTION 03 / MECHANISM

What 5 billion CFUs
actually do.

Beneficial bacteria don't work by magic. They survive, colonize, and produce. Here's the three-step pathway from chew to gut wall.

STEP 01 T = 0–60 min

Survive stomach acid.

Strains are selected and microencapsulated to survive low-pH gastric conditions and reach the small intestine alive.

pH 2.0 → 6.5 ≥ 90% viability
STEP 02 T = 1–24 hr

Colonize the gut wall.

Probiotics adhere to the intestinal mucosa, occupy binding sites, and outcompete opportunistic pathogens.

colonies × 10⁶/g + adhesion 4–8×
STEP 03 T = 24+ hr

Produce SCFAs.

Fermentation of fibers produces short-chain fatty acids — butyrate, acetate, propionate — which support epithelial cells, stool quality, and immune signaling.

butyrate ↑ + immune IL-10 ↑
